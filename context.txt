Please tell us about a time you most successfully hacked some (non-computer) system to your advantage.*
While building Bloomify, I needed a large dataset of exam questions to train on. Our university's question papers were publicly available on their website but scattered across hundreds of PDFs with no structured access. So I scraped the entire site, pulled every past paper I could find, and ran OCR across them to build a bank of 12,000+ questions spanning several years.

While cleaning the dataset, I noticed a pattern: a meaningful subset of questions repeated across years, often with only minor wording changes. I built an ML model to formalize this ,  it analyzed historical trends to predict which questions were likely to appear in upcoming exams, and generated variations of each to improve coverage across different phrasings.

It worked.
